The legal choice for complex litigation

The problem of choice of law in complex litigation did not receive much attention until the late 1980s0 A variety of factors contributed to the delay. Tort and product liability law did not offer substantive theories on which to ground such litigation much before the 1970s, and the procedural tools for constructing complex, multi-jurisdictional lawsuits are also relatively recent innovations. Class action litigation, for example, was generally limited prior to the 1966 amendments to the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure and took several more years to gather momentum.
